Pt: Thomas Reynard MC #8472059
Date: 02/18/25

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
The patient is a 65-year-old man with a history of type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease (stage 3) who presented to the Emergency Department on Tuesday with complaints of worsening fatigue, generalized weakness, and swelling in his lower extremities over the past week. He reported difficulty breathing when lying flat and a recent 5-pound weight gain despite no significant changes in diet. He admitted to missing his last two dialysis sessions due to transportation issues. The patient was admitted to the hospital for evaluation and management of suspected fluid overload and uremia. Nephrologist Dr. Patel was consulted.

SOCIAL HISTORY:
Lives with his wife in Madison, WI. Retired teacher. Does not smoke or drink alcohol. Hobbies include gardening and playing chess.

HOSPITAL COURSE AND TREATMENT:
During admission, the patient was seen by Nephrology, Dr. Patel, and was started on IV Lasix 40 mg b.i.d. with strict fluid restrictions and a low-sodium diet. Labs revealed a potassium level of 5.6 mmol/L, and he was given Kayexalate. Dialysis was resumed the following day, with removal of 2.5 liters of fluid. By 02/20/25, the patient reported improved breathing and reduced swelling. On 02/22/25, he was medically stable and discharged home with instructions to resume his regular dialysis schedule and follow up in the nephrology clinic within one week.
