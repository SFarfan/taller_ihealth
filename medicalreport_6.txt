Pt: Daniel Prescott MC #3029847
Date: 11/10/25

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
The patient is a 59-year-old man with a history of coronary artery disease and type 2 diabetes who presented to the Emergency Department on Monday with chest discomfort and shortness of breath that began during moderate physical activity earlier in the day. He described the discomfort as a squeezing sensation in the middle of his chest, radiating to his left shoulder, and rated it as 7/10 in intensity. The symptoms resolved with rest but recurred intermittently over the past week. He denied nausea or syncope. Initial EKG showed no ST changes, but troponin levels were mildly elevated. The patient was admitted for evaluation and management of unstable angina, with Cardiologist Dr. Miller to follow.

SOCIAL HISTORY:
Lives with his wife in Cleveland, OH. Works as an accountant. Former smoker (quit 10 years ago), drinks alcohol occasionally. Enjoys woodworking and gardening.

HOSPITAL COURSE AND TREATMENT:
During admission, the patient underwent a coronary angiography, which revealed a 75% stenosis in the left anterior descending artery. Dr. Miller performed a percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) with drug-eluting stent placement. Post-procedure, the patient was started on dual antiplatelet therapy with aspirin 81 mg q.d. and clopidogrel 75 mg q.d. along with atorvastatin 80 mg q.d. and metoprolol 50 mg b.i.d. By 11/12/25, the patient was symptom-free, with stable vitals and improved exercise tolerance. On 11/13/25, he was discharged home with lifestyle modification counseling and a referral to cardiac rehabilitation. Follow-up with Cardiology is scheduled in two weeks.
